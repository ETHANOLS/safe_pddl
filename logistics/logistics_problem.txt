This is a package delivery problem involving multiple cities.

There are 6 cities: city1, city2, city3, city4, city5, and city6.
Each city has two locations:
- one regular location (like city1-1)
- one airport (like city1-2)

Each location belongs to its city, and trucks can only move within the same city. Airplanes can travel between airports in different cities.

There are two airplanes, plane1 and plane2. Both of them are initially located at the airport in city4 (city4-2).

Each city has one truck:
- truck1 is at city1-1
- truck2 is at city2-1
- truck3 is at city3-1
- truck4 is at city4-1
- truck5 is at city5-1
- truck6 is at city6-1

There are 6 packages:
- package1 is at city2-1
- package2 is at city1-2
- package3 is at city1-1
- package4 is at city1-1
- package5 is at city4-2
- package6 is at city3-1

You can move packages using the following actions:
- A package can be loaded into a truck or airplane if they are at the same location.
- A package can be unloaded from a vehicle at its current location.
- Trucks can drive between locations within the same city.
- Airplanes can fly between airports in different cities.

The goal is to move the packages so that:
- package6 is at city1-2
- package5 is at city6-2
- package4 is at city3-2
- package3 is at city6-1
- package2 is at city6-2
- package1 stays at city2-1
